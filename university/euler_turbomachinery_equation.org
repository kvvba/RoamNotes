#+title: Euler turbomachinery equation

The Euler equation applies to all types of turbomachinery.

For a turbine, the Euler equations will give a negative value, for a compressor it will give a positive value.

The power input/output is given entirely by the change of [[id:edc1334d-4a8e-42ff-abd7-6fbd35aa5895][Angular momentum]]. Changes in radius will also influence the power input/output.

w = \omega (V_2\theta r_2 - V_1\theta r_1)

Where
w is specific work
\omega is rotational speed
V is the tangential velocity
r is the radius

This can be related to [[id:e1eae400-70c7-4e53-83a8-ad642e35553d][Stagnation temperature]] change (and hence [[id:4bc4518b-9f85-4916-b6b6-de492344372f][Stagnation pressure]] change)

w = \omega (V_2\theta r_2 - V_1\theta r_1) = c_p (T_2 - T_1)
